# Princeton Plasma Physics Group — Mikhailova

## Overview

The plasma physics group in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Princeton University, led by Julia M. Mikhailova, focuses on theoretical and computational studies of relativistic laser-plasma interactions for attosecond source development.

## Key Researchers

- **Julia M. Mikhailova** — Group leader, theoretical/computational
- **Matthew R. Edwards** — Former PhD student (now at LLNL)
- **Nicholas M. Fasano** — PhD student

## Research Focus

1. **Waveform engineering**: Optimal driving waveforms for HHG
2. **Scaling laws**: Theoretical foundations of HHG efficiency
3. **Thin-foil HHG**: Nanoscale target optimization
4. **Two-color enhancement**: Phase-dependent HHG enhancement

## Key Contributions

### 2014: Two-color HHG enhancement (first demonstration)
- 5% SH → 10× attosecond intensity
- Edwards, Platonenko, Mikhailova, OL 39, 6823 (2014)

### 2016: Waveform-controlled HHG (definitive theory)
- Optimal waveform: single oscillation at ω_pR
- 63% conversion efficiency limit
- Genetic algorithm optimization
- Edwards & Mikhailova, PRL 117, 125001 (2016)

### 2019: Edwards thesis (comprehensive study)
- Scaling laws: p ∝ ln(a₀/N), not universal
- Bunch-width limited cutoff
- Secondary attosecond pulses
- Thin-foil n^{-10/3} scaling

### 2020: Thin-foil HHG theory
- Electron nanobunch width dominates spectrum
- Edwards, Fasano, Mikhailova, PRL 124, 185004 (2020)
