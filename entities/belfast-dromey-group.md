# Queen's University Belfast — Dromey Group

## Overview

The Centre for Light Matter Interactions at Queen's University Belfast, led by Brendan Dromey, is a leading experimental group in relativistic high-order harmonic generation and attosecond science.

## Key Researchers

- **Brendan Dromey** — Group leader, experimental HHG
- **Mark Yeung** — Two-color HHG experiments
- **Matt Zepf** — Senior researcher (also at Helmholtz Institut Jena)

## Research Focus

1. **Relativistic HHG experiments**: ROM, CSE, efficiency measurements
2. **Two-color enhancement**: Experimental demonstration of waveform effects
3. **Diffraction-limited harmonics**: Spatial quality of harmonic beams
4. **Attosecond pulse characterization**: Temporal diagnostics

## Key Contributions

### 2006: First HHG scaling law measurement
- Measured exponent p = -2.5(+0.2, -0.3)
- Consistent with both Gordienko and Baeva predictions
- Dromey et al., Nat. Phys. 2, 456 (2006)

### 2007: Cutoff frequency verification
- Confirmed γ³ scaling (not 4γ²)
- Decisive test of BGP theory
- Dromey et al., PRL 99, 085001 (2007)

### 2009: Diffraction-limited harmonics
- Demonstrated spatial coherence of HHG
- Focused harmonics to micron spots
- Dromey et al., Nat. Phys. 5, 146 (2009)

### 2016: Two-color HHG experiment
- First experimental demonstration of phase-dependent enhancement
- Yeung et al., PRL (2016)

### 2026: Efficiency-optimized HHG (Nature)
- First verification of theoretical maximum efficiency
- 0.17% conversion, 9.5 mJ in H12-H47
- Timmis et al., Nature (2026)
