# MPQ — Max-Planck-Institut für Quantenoptik

## Overview

The Max-Planck-Institut für Quantenoptik (MPQ) in Garching, Germany, is a premier research institute for quantum optics and laser physics. It has played a foundational role in the development of relativistic HHG theory and experiments.

## Historical Contributions to HHG

### 1996: ROM fluid model
- Lichters (PhD student at TU Munich), Meyer-ter-Vehn, Pukhov
- Developed full fluid equations for ROM
- Created LPIC++ PIC code
- Lichters, Meyer-ter-Vehn, Pukhov, Phys. Plasmas 3, 3425 (1996)

### 2004: Gordienko's breakthrough
- Gordienko (Landau Institute, visiting Pukhov at HHU Düsseldorf)
- Saddle-point analysis of ROM
- Found I_n ∝ n^{-5/2}, cutoff at 4γ²_max ω_L
- Gordienko et al., PRL 93, 115002 (2004)

### 2005: Similarity theory
- Gordienko and Pukhov
- Self-similar parameter S = n_e/(n_c a₀)
- Gordienko, Pukhov, Phys. Plasmas 12, 043109 (2005)

### 2006: BGP theory (definitive)
- Baeva (Pukhov's PhD student)
- γ-spike model, n^{-8/3} scaling
- Baeva, Gordienko, Pukhov, PRE 74, 046404 (2006)

## Related Facilities

### ATLAS Laser
- Used by Heissler (2012 thesis)
- Few-cycle, high-energy pulses
- Isolated attosecond pulse experiments

### LWS-20 (Light Wave Synthesizer 20)
- Few-cycle laser for HHG experiments
- Used for first ROM experiments with few-cycle driver

## Legacy

### Codes Developed
- LPIC++ → basis for many subsequent PIC codes
- Influence on EPOCH, BOPS, and other codes

### Theory Foundation
- BGP theory remains the standard ROM model
- Similarity theory widely used for scaling predictions
- γ-spike concept fundamental to HHG understanding

### Experimental Demonstrations
- First systematic HHG scaling measurements
- Isolated attosecond pulse from plasma mirrors (Heissler 2012)

## Current Status

The original MPQ HHG group has dispersed:
- Pukhov → HHU Düsseldorf (continued theory)
- Zepf → Queen's University Belfast (experiments)
- Dromey → Belfast (experiments)
- Kiefer → MPQ (electron mirrors)
