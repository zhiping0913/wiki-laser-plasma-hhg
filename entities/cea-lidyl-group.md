# CEA-LIDYL (Quéré & Vincenti Group)

## Overview

The Physics at High Intensity group at CEA-LIDYL (Lasers, Interactions and Dynamics Laboratory) in Saclay, France, is a leading research group in relativistic plasma mirrors and attosecond source development.

## Key Researchers

- **Fabien Quéré** — Senior researcher, group leader
- **Henri Vincenti** — Theoretical/numerical work on plasma mirrors
- **S. Monchocé** — Experimental work
- **S. Kahaly** — Experimental work

## Research Focus

1. **Relativistic plasma mirrors**: Physics of PM dynamics, curvature, harmonic generation
2. **Path to Schwinger limit**: Curved relativistic plasma mirrors for extreme intensities
3. **Attosecond lighthouse**: Techniques for isolated attosecond pulse generation
4. **High-order harmonic generation**: ROM, CWE mechanisms, efficiency optimization

## Key Contributions

### 2014: PM curvature model
- Analytical model of laser-induced PM denting
- Electron/ion dynamics separation
- Experimental validation with harmonic divergence measurements
- Vincenti et al., Nat. Commun. 5, 3414 (2014)

### 2020: Attosecond lighthouse techniques
- Wavefront curvature compensation for divergence reduction
- Amplitude profile tailoring
- 3D PIC validation of isolated attosecond pulses
- Kallala, Quéré, Vincenti, PRR 2, 043007 (2020)

### 2021: Path to Schwinger limit (review)
- Comprehensive review of CRM concept
- State-of-the-art analysis of feasibility
- Quéré & Vincenti, HPLSE 9, e6 (2021)

### 2023: Theoretical and numerical developments
- Pseudo-spectral PIC codes (PICSAR, WarpX)
- 3D simulations of CRM
- Vincenti et al., EPJST 232, 2303 (2023)
